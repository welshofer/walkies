The River of Time

London, England

From medieval ruins to modern art, Borough Market to St. Paul's. Follow the Thames through a thousand years of history.

Audio Guide

Before You Go

Best start time: 9:30 AM Wednesday to Saturday for Borough Market
Start point: Tower Hill Station (Circle/District Lines)
Weather: Can reach 30C in summer, good shelter on route
Budget: Free walking, Lunch 20 to 40 GBP

1

St. Dunstan in the East

20 minutes St Dunstan's Hill, EC3R 5DD

A secret garden hidden inside the shell of a bombed-out medieval church. This is one of the most atmospheric spots in London, and most tourists walk right past it.

Look for the trees growing through the arched windows of the Wren-designed tower. The contrast between the grey stone ruins and the lush green vines is stunning.

Originally built around 1100, damaged in the Great Fire of 1666, patched up by Christopher Wren, and destroyed in the Blitz in 1941. The City turned it into a public garden in 1971.

Insider Tip

It opens at 8 AM. If you start your tour early, you might have this enchanting space entirely to yourself for photos.

Walk to Stop 2
400 meters 5 minutes

Exit the garden and head south towards the river. Turn left onto Lower Thames Street, passing the ancient Roman Wall fragments, until the massive fortress walls dominate your view.

Tower of London

30 minutes Tower Hill, EC3N 4AB

A UNESCO World Heritage site and the most secure castle in the land for nearly 1,000 years. It has been a palace, fortress, prison, and zoo. Built by William the Conqueror in the 1070s.

Look for Traitors' Gate from the embankment side, where prisoners like Anne Boleyn entered by water. Watch for the ravens on the lawn; legend says if they leave, the Kingdom falls.

Insider Tip

You don't need a ticket to see the best views. Walk the riverside path for a perfect view of the White Tower and medieval walls without the crowds inside.

Walk to Stop 3
200 meters 5 minutes

Continue east along the riverfront. You cannot miss the massive bridge structure ahead. Walk up the stairs to the bridge deck level.

3

Tower Bridge

20 minutes Tower Bridge Road, SE1 2UP

The world's most famous bascule bridge. A Victorian engineering marvel opened in 1894. Look for the bascules at the base of the towers and the river churning below through the pedestrian railing gaps.

In 1952, a bus driver jumped his double-decker across a 3-foot gap when the bridge started opening while he was still on it. He made it.

Insider Tip

Check the Bridge Lift Times online before you go. Seeing the bridge open for a large ship is a lucky bonus.

Walk to Stop 4
500 meters 10 minutes

Once across the bridge, descend the stairs to the right and walk under the bridge arch. You are entering Butler's Wharf, the historic warehouse district.

4

Shad Thames

15 minutes Shad Thames, SE1 2YT

This historic cobblestone street looks exactly like 19th-century industrial London. It was the Larder of London where tea, coffee, and spices were stored.

Look for the wrought-iron overhead gantries connecting the warehouses. These rolled barrels between buildings without going down to the muddy street. A favorite film location for Bridget Jones and Cruella.

Insider Tip

The acoustics here are incredible. Your footsteps will echo off the brick canyons.

Walk to Stop 5
1.2 kilometers 15 minutes

Walk west along the Thames path, passing HMS Belfast. Continue past Hay's Galleria with its kinetic sculpture until you see the railway bridges and the market entrance.

5

Borough Market

60 minutes (lunch) 8 Southwark Street, SE1 1TL

London's oldest and most renowned food market, dating back to the 12th century. Look for the Market Bell and the Art Deco green glass structure. Monks traded grain here in the Middle Ages.

Insider Tip

Do not commit to the first thing you see. Do a tasting lap first. Most vendors offer free samples of cheese and charcuterie.

What to Eat

The Ginger Pig for the best sausage rolls in London. Kappacasein for raclette over potatoes. Padella for fresh pasta if the queue is short. Monmouth Coffee for a flat white.

Walk to Stop 6
200 meters 3 minutes

Exit the market onto Borough High Street. Look for a narrow alleyway entrance leading to a courtyard.

Open in Google Maps
6

The George Inn

30 minutes 75-77 Borough High Street, SE1 1NH

The last remaining galleried coaching inn in London, owned by the National Trust. Look for the wooden galleries overlooking the courtyard. This was the bus terminal of the 1600s.

Charles Dickens was a regular here. He mentions it in Little Dorrit.

Insider Tip

Even if you do not drink, step into the Parliament Bar to see the ancient fireplaces and uneven floors.

Walk to Stop 7
600 meters 8 minutes

Walk back toward the river, past the Golden Hinde replica ship. Follow Clink Street past the old prison to the riverside.

Shakespeare's Globe

15 minutes 21 New Globe Walk, SE1 9DT

A faithful reconstruction of the open-air playhouse where Shakespeare worked. The thatched roof is the only one allowed in Central London since the Great Fire. The original burned in 1613 when a cannon misfired during Henry VIII.

Insider Tip

You can see the groundlings standing in the yard through the iron gates if a rehearsal is happening.

Walk to Stop 8
100 meters 2 minutes

Right next door is the massive brick power station turned gallery.

8

Tate Modern

30 minutes Bankside, SE1 9TG

The world's most popular modern art gallery, housed in the former Bankside Power Station. Look for the Turbine Hall, a cavernous industrial space that hosts massive art installations.

Insider Tip

Head to Level 10 of the Blavatnik Building for a 360-degree view of London. Often less crowded than the Shard and usually free.

Walk to Stop 9
50 meters 1 minute

Exit the Tate on the river side. The metal bridge is directly in front of you.

Open in Google Maps
9

Millennium Bridge

15 minutes Thames Embankment

The Wobbly Bridge. A pedestrian Blade of Light connecting Tate Modern to St Paul's. Opened in 2000, closed 2 days later due to swaying, reopened 2 years later.

Look for the tiny chewing gum art on the bridge deck. Artist Ben Wilson paints miniature scenes on discarded gum.

Photo Op

This is the best angle for St. Paul's Cathedral. Frame the dome perfectly between the bridge supports.

Walk to Stop 10
400 meters 5 minutes

Walk straight off the bridge, up Peter's Hill towards the massive dome.

Open in Google Maps
10

St. Paul's Cathedral

End of tour St Paul's Churchyard, EC4M 8AD

Sir Christopher Wren's masterpiece and the spiritual heart of the nation. Rebuilt after the Great Fire of 1666, it survived the Blitz while the area around it burned. Look for the pineapples on the western towers, symbols of hospitality.

This is where Prince Charles married Lady Diana, and where Winston Churchill had his funeral.

Insider Tip

The One New Change shopping center next door has a free public roof terrace with a stunning eye-level view of the Dome.

The End of Your Walk

You have walked through a thousand years of London history, from medieval church ruins to Victorian engineering marvels, from Shakespeare's stage to modern art. The Thames has witnessed it all, and now so have you.
