The Hunt for Gold & Gothic Shadows

Alba, Italy · November

Medieval towers, the world's most precious fungus, and the heart of Piedmont wine country during the legendary White Truffle Fair.

Before You Go

Best start time: 10:00 AM to hit the truffle fair early
Start point: Piazza Michele Ferrero (5 min from train station)
Weather: 4 to 12 degrees Celsius, foggy mornings
Budget: Lunch: 50 to 80 euros. Truffle: 300+ euros.

Your Route

1

Piazza Michele Ferrero

10 minutes Piazza Michele Ferrero, 12051 Alba CN

This elegant square is the living room of modern Alba and the gateway to the medieval old town. It marks the transition from the 19th-century city to the historic core that lies just ahead.

The piazza was recently renamed after Michele Ferrero of Nutella fame, Alba's most famous industrialist and benefactor. The scent of roasting hazelnuts from the nearby Ferrero factory sometimes drifts across the square, a reminder that you are in the heart of hazelnut country.

Look for the massive grape bunch sculptures often displayed here, signaling the city's wine heritage. Locals still often call it Piazza Savona, the old name, so if you ask for directions, you might hear both.

Insider Tip

Before leaving the square, grab a quick caffe al banco, a standing espresso, at Caffe Calissano on the corner. It is an institution. Ordering at the bar is cheaper than sitting at a table, and it is how the locals do it.

2

Cortile della Maddalena

45 minutes Via Vittorio Emanuele II, 19

This is Ground Zero for the International Alba White Truffle Fair, the Fiera Internazionale del Tartufo Bianco d'Alba. Inside the Mercato Mondiale, you can smell, touch, and buy certified white truffles, the most expensive fungus in the world.

The complex was originally a convent for Dominican nuns, later an orphanage. Now it houses the heart of the truffle trade. Record-breaking truffles have sold for over 100,000 euros at charity auctions held here, with bidders calling in from Hong Kong and New York.

Visit the Truffle Sensory Analysis booth. Even if you do not buy, watch the judges weighing and checking the quality of the tubers. The intense, earthy aroma is unforgettable, a mix of garlic, honey, and ancient forest floor.

Truffle Buying Tip

If you buy a truffle, demand the numbered sack. It tracks the truffle back to the seller, guaranteeing quality. This is a right established by the fair's commission. Expect to pay 300 to 600 euros for a decent-sized nugget, roughly 4,000 to 6,000 euros per kilogram.

Best Times

Go early around 10 AM to beat the crowds, or late afternoon around 4 PM when the aroma is strongest. On Sundays, look for the folklore events with medieval re-enactments and flag throwers, the Sbandieratori.

3

Chiesa di San Domenico

15 minutes Via Teobaldo Calissano, 12051 Alba CN

A stunning example of early Italian Gothic architecture that was nearly destroyed by Napoleon's troops, who used it as a stable for their horses. Built in the 13th century, it is no longer a consecrated church but serves as a prestigious exhibition space.

Look for the frescoes on the pillars, which were only rediscovered in the 20th century under layers of whitewash applied during one of the building's less glorious incarnations. During the Truffle Fair, it often hosts high-end art installations.

During the plague of 1630, this church was one of the few places residents would go to pray for salvation, leaving graffiti that is still faintly visible in corners.

Insider Tip

The acoustics inside are perfect. If a choir is practicing, step inside immediately. The sound reverberates through the Gothic arches in a way that modern concert halls spend millions trying to replicate.

4

Alba Underground

45 minutes Piazza Risorgimento, 2 (Tourist Office)

Alba is a city upon a city. The Roman city of Alba Pompeia lies two to three meters below your feet. The medieval city was built directly on top of the Roman one, preserving temple foundations and roads underground in a remarkable layer cake of history.

Look for glass panels in the pavement in various spots, like Piazza Pertinace, that look down into Roman ruins. The Alba Sotterranea guided tours, highly recommended for adventurous types, take you into the bowels of the city to see ancient Roman gatehouses and mosaic floors.

Advance Booking

Book the Alba Underground tour in advance for a specific time slot. Tours are popular during the truffle fair. If you cannot get a ticket, visit the Federico Eusebio Museum nearby to see the excavated artifacts and get a sense of the Roman city that lies beneath your feet.

5

Piazza Risorgimento and the Duomo

30 minutes Piazza Risorgimento, 12051 Alba CN

The medieval heartbeat of Alba. This square is dominated by the Cattedrale di San Lorenzo and the Town Hall. It has been the center of political and religious life since Roman times. Look for the Three Towers skyline visible from the corners of the square: Sineo, Bonino, and Astesiano.

The cathedral is known for its distinct red brick facade and deep blue vaulted ceilings with gold stars, reminiscent of the Basilica in Assisi. Inside, look for the wood-carved choir stalls behind the altar, an intricately detailed masterpiece from the Renaissance.

Built on top of a Roman temple, the current structure is a mix of Romanesque, Gothic, and Baroque renovations. Legend says the Holy Thorn from Christ's crown is kept here, though it is rarely displayed. The bell tower houses the San Lorenzo bell, which rings a specific peal for the opening of the Truffle Fair.

Insider Tip

It is often colder inside the cathedral than outside in November. Keep your coat on. Look for the bas-reliefs on the Town Hall facade across the square, depicting varied scenes of local history.

6

Piazza Pertinace

15 minutes Piazza Elvio Pertinace, 12051 Alba CN

A quieter counterpart to Piazza Duomo, historically the market of the peasants. Look for the statue of Publius Helvius Pertinax, a Roman Emperor born in Alba. He reigned for only 87 days during the Year of the Five Emperors in 193 AD before being assassinated by his own guards.

This square was the site of a silk market in the 1800s. Now it feels much more local. This is a great place to spot residents walking their dogs or chatting over coffee, away from the tourist crowds of Via Maestra. The Castellaccio ruins are visible nearby.

Wine Break

Voglia di Vino is located just off this square on Via Elvio Pertinace. It has a cave-like atmosphere in the cellar and extremely knowledgeable staff who love explaining the terroir. Order a flight of Barolo versus Barbaresco, both made from Nebbiolo grapes but grown on different soils with very different characters.

F

Osteria dell'Arco

Lunch: 1.5 hours Piazza Savona 5 (in a courtyard)

A Slow Food osteria that fits the active, adventurous profile perfectly: high quality but unpretentious, lively, and strictly traditional. It adheres to the Slow Food philosophy of good, clean, and fair. The restaurant is tucked into a courtyard off the piazza, away from the noise but close to the action.

This is where you experience the truffle in its full glory. The staff will bring the truffle to your table and shave it fresh over your dish, releasing that intoxicating aroma.

What to Order

Tajarin, the thin Piedmontese egg noodles, with fresh white truffle shavings. This is the definitive Alba dish during truffle season. Start with Vitello Tonnato, cold veal with tuna sauce, and pair everything with a Barolo or Barbaresco. Expect 50 to 80 euros per person.

The End of Your Walk

You have walked through layers of history, from Roman Alba Pompeia to medieval towers to the modern temple of the white truffle. The nebbia, the famous Piedmont fog, may be rolling in by now. It is perfect weather for a glass of Barolo and a plate of tajarin. The train station is a five-minute walk from Piazza Michele Ferrero where you began.
