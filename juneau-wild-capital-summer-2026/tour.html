The Golden Loop

Juneau, Alaska · August

From gold rush history to temperate rainforest wilderness, climb through Alaska's capital to discover spawning salmon, Russian heritage, and a mining valley where nature is reclaiming the past.

Audio Guide

Before You Go

Best start time: 9:00 AM (beat the cruise crowds)
Start point: Seawalk near Tahku Whale Sculpture
Weather: Liquid sunshine. Bring rain gear.
Budget: Lunch: 25 to 60 dollars. Museum: 5 dollars.

Your Route

Tour stops Food stop Walking route
1

Tahku (The Whale Project)

20 minutes Overstreet Park, W 8th St & Egan Dr

Your journey begins at a stunning life-sized bronze sculpture of a breaching humpback whale rising from an infinity pool. It perfectly captures the scale of the wildlife you hope to see during your Alaska adventure.

The sculpture commemorates the 50th anniversary of Alaska statehood. Stand here on the Seawalk and take in your first views of Gastineau Channel, the waterway that brought gold seekers to these shores over a century ago.

Wildlife Spotting

Look up at the light poles. Bald eagles often perch here, surveying the channel for fish. Scan the water for harbor seals bobbing near the docks.

Photo Op

Stand near the blowhole of the sculpture. It occasionally sprays water simulating a whale spout, making for a fun action photo with the mountains behind.

Walk to Stop 2
800 meters 15 minutes

Head south along the waterfront Seawalk, then turn left uphill on Gold Street. Prepare for your first steep climb. Juneau is built on a mountainside.

2

St. Nicholas Russian Orthodox Church

15 minutes 326 5th St, Juneau, AK 99801

Built in 1893, this octagonal structure with its golden onion dome is one of Juneau's most photographed icons and a testament to the lingering Russian influence in Alaska. It has been in continuous use since the Gold Rush era.

The church was built largely at the request of the local Tlingit community who had converted to Russian Orthodoxy during the Russian colonial period. Look for the intricate wooden architecture and peek into the small gift shop if it is open.

Photo Op

The best angle is from the street corner looking up at the dome against the backdrop of Mount Juneau. The contrast of the golden dome against the green mountain is striking.

Walk to Stop 3
500 meters 10 minutes

Walk west on 5th Street, then turn right uphill on Calhoun Avenue. Continue climbing.

3

The Governor's Mansion

10 minutes 716 Calhoun Ave, Juneau, AK 99801

A stately colonial-style home built in 1912. This is the people's house of Alaska, sitting impressively on the hillside overlooking the channel. The mansion has hosted presidents and dignitaries throughout its history.

Look for the Liberty Totem Pole standing prominently in the front yard, a rare sight for a governor's residence. It was carved by Tlingit master carvers and represents the connection between Alaska's Native heritage and modern government.

Insider Tip

You cannot go inside, but the view of the channel from the sidewalk across the street is excellent. This elevated vantage point offers a different perspective on the waterfront you walked along earlier.

Walk to Stop 4
500 meters 10 minutes

Continue on Calhoun, then descend the stairs and path into the park valley. You are entering the rainforest.

4

Cope Park & Gold Creek

20 minutes Cope Park Access Rd, Juneau, AK

This is where the city meets the wilderness. It is a lush, green canyon park centered around Gold Creek, the exact spot where Joe Juneau and Richard Harris first found gold in 1880, sparking the rush that built this city.

In August, look into the creek for Chum salmon returning to spawn. Their journey from the ocean to this creek is one of nature's great dramas. Also watch for American Dippers, grey birds that remarkably walk underwater along the creek bottom hunting for insects.

Wildlife Spotting

August is prime salmon run season. Watch the creek carefully for salmon fighting their way upstream. Their silvery flashes and splashing are unmistakable.

Walk to Stop 5
1.6 kilometers 30 minutes

The active segment. Walk UP Basin Road, a shared dirt and gravel road that winds up the canyon. It is scenic and peaceful, but be aware that black bears are common here. Make noise by talking loudly as you walk.

Open in Google Maps
5

Last Chance Basin Mining Museum

30 minutes 1001 Basin Rd, Juneau, AK 99801

You are now in a historic mining valley surrounded by mountains. The old compressor building, now a museum, is a rustic industrial relic being reclaimed by nature. This was the operational heart of the Alaska-Juneau Gold Mining Company.

Look for the massive wooden trestle bridge you cross to get here. Waterfalls cascade down the mountainsides, including Ebner Falls. The combination of industrial archaeology and wild nature is striking.

Insider Tip

Even if you do not pay to enter the museum (5 dollars cash usually), the area outside offers incredible ghost town vibes and mountain views. This is your turnaround point.

Photo Op

The trestle bridge framed against the waterfalls and green mountains makes for a dramatic photograph. The overcast light common in August is actually perfect for capturing the deep greens.

Walk to Stop 6
1.6 kilometers 25 minutes

Head back DOWN Basin Road. Enjoy the easy downhill stroll and different perspective on the rainforest canyon.

6

Alaska State Capitol

15 minutes 120 4th St, Juneau, AK 99801

It might be the only state capitol that looks like a generic office building, but it is full of character. Juneau has fought hard to remain the capital despite numerous votes to move it to a more accessible location.

Look for the marble pillars made from Alaskan marble and the artwork in the lobby depicting Alaska's history and landscapes.

Insider Tip

It is open to the public on weekdays. You can usually just walk into the lobby to dry off from the rain and see the historical displays. A warm respite if the weather turns.

Walk to Stop 7
300 meters 5 minutes

Walk down the hill to South Franklin Street, the historic heart of tourist Juneau.

7

Historic South Franklin Street

20 minutes S Franklin St, Juneau, AK

The beating heart of tourist Juneau. Victorian-era facades, saloons, and shops line this historic street. This was the rough-and-tumble center of the gold rush town where miners came to spend their earnings.

Look for the Red Dog Saloon with its swinging doors and sawdust floor. You do not have to eat there, but peek inside to see the Wyatt Earp gun and soak in the frontier atmosphere.

Insider Tip

Look up at the mountains straight above you. That is Mount Roberts. You can take the tramway up for spectacular views, though the walk you just completed offered its own mountain experience.

F

Tracy's King Crab Shack

Lunch: 45 minutes 432 S Franklin St

Right at the end of your tour, this is THE place to eat in Juneau. Yes, it is expensive. Yes, there is usually a line. But the Alaskan King Crab is world-famous for a reason.

The outdoor picnic table seating overlooking the harbor is part of the experience. Watch floatplanes take off while cracking crab legs.

What to Order

The Combo Number 1: King Crab leg, crab bisque, and crab cakes. Budget 40 to 60 dollars per person. For a more casual option, try Deckhand Dave's fish tacos in the food truck lot at 139 South Franklin Street for 18 to 25 dollars.

The End of Your Walk

You have climbed through Alaska's capital from the waterfront to the rainforest and back, following the path of gold seekers and salmon alike. From the Russian heritage church to the ghost town mining valley, from spawning salmon in Gold Creek to King Crab on South Franklin Street, this is Juneau's golden loop. The cruise ships will depart, but you have walked where the prospectors walked and seen what draws people to this wild capital at the edge of the world.
