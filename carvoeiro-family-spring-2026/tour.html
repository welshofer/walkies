Algar Seco

20 min Estr. do Algar Seco 16, 8400-512 Carvoeiro

Start your adventure at this wonderland of wind-carved limestone cliffs, grottoes, and natural pools. These Miocene limestone formations were created over 20 million years ago from ancient marine fossils.

What to Look For

The star attraction is the Boneca, which means The Doll. It is a cave formation that looks like the inside of a doll's dress. Inside, there are two windows carved into the rock looking out to sea, creating the perfect photo frame.

The Legend of Princess Alfanzina

A Moorish princess fell in love with a local charcoal maker, which is where Carvoeiro gets its name. They met secretly in these caves. When her father discovered the relationship and killed her lover, she cried so many tears at this spot that they say her tears carved the winding channels in the rocks.

For the Kids

This place feels like a natural playground! The Boneca cave is an easy, safe adventure for the boys to explore. Can they find the two windows that look out to sea? Stand inside and wave to the rest of the family outside.

Safety Note

Walk down the main stairs to the Boneca bar level. The cave entrance is just to the right of the bar. There are handrails on the stairs, but once you step onto the natural rocks there are no rails. Keep a hand on the 5-year-old.

Photo Op

The Doll Window is the iconic shot. Position yourself inside the cave looking out through the rock windows to the Atlantic. The natural frame creates a dramatic portrait.

Walk to Fort
600 meters 10 minutes

From the Algar Seco parking lot, look for the wooden walkway entrance. Do NOT walk on the road. This flat, safe wooden boardwalk is perched on top of the cliffs.

Look down to see birds nesting in the cliff face and boats entering hidden caves below. The boardwalk is perfect for seniors and kids with no cars and no tripping hazards.

Forte de Nossa Senhora da Encarnacao

10 min R. da Rampa Sra. da Encarnacao, 8400-516 Carvoeiro

This historic lookout point marks the end of the boardwalk. The white walls against the blue sky make for a classic Algarve picture.

History

Built in the 1670s to defend the town from pirates and corsairs who constantly raided this tuna-fishing village. The original fort was destroyed by the devastating 1755 earthquake, but the archway and a small 17th-century chapel remain.

The Pirate Battle of 1544

This coast was a battleground between Portuguese defenders and Ottoman pirates. In 1544, the Portuguese captain Pedro da Cunha fought the Turkish pirate Xarramet right off this coast. This fort was built because of battles like that one.

For the Kids

Pirates! Real pirates attacked this coast, and this fort was built to stop them. Can the boys imagine standing guard here, watching for pirate ships on the horizon? What would they do if they spotted one?

Grandparent Rest Stop

There is a small playground just behind the fort if the kids need to burn off 5 minutes of energy while the adults admire the view and catch their breath.

Photo Op

The white fort walls against the deep blue Atlantic sky is the classic Algarve postcard shot. Best lighting in the morning or late afternoon.

Walk to Beach Square
350 meters 10 minutes

Take the Rampa da Sra. da Encarnacao, a paved ramp leading down from the Fort to the beach.

This is the postcard shot of Carvoeiro! The view of the beach and white village houses stacked on the cliff from this ramp is spectacular. Take it slow as it is downhill. Look for the colorful fishing boats parked on the sand below.

Praia do Carvoeiro

15 min Largo da Praia, 8400-517 Carvoeiro

The beating heart of the village. This small, manageable square opens directly onto the sand where colorful fishing boats are pulled up on the beach.

History

Carvoeiro was originally a small fishing village. The name comes from carvoeiro meaning charcoal maker. The hills above once supplied charcoal to fuel the fishing boats.

For the Kids

Direct beach access for quick toe-dipping in the Atlantic! Look for the Carvoeiro boat sign often placed for photos. The cliffs shelter this cove, making it warmer and less windy than the boardwalk above.

Family Portrait

Walk 20 feet onto the sand. With the colorful boats behind you and the white cliff houses above, this is the perfect family photo spot.

Return to Parking: The Shopping Loop
850 meters 15-20 minutes

To complete the loop, walk up Rua dos Pescadores, the main shopping street leading away from the beach, then turn right onto Estrada do Algar Seco.

This is an uphill walk to get back to the car. However, Rua dos Pescadores is a gentle incline lined with pottery shops and cork stores, making it easy to distract from the climb.

Grandparent Option

If the grandparents are tired, have one adult walk up to get the car (10 minutes fast walk) while the family waits at the beach square. Alternatively, grab a Tuk-Tuk from the square for approximately 5 to 10 euros.
